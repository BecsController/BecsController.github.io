What learnings did you take from the chapter?


I loved the scientific explanations of things that can be seen as new age thinking and therefore less credible. it’s not just woo-woo talk by some New Age person; it is something so tangible, you can see it and actually measure it with a ruler. In particular, the explanations of gut thinking and intuition. I liked the idea that the default state for the mind is happiness. That happiness is not something that you pursue; it is something you allow. Whatever it is that you are experiencing, just experience it. Do not judge it to be good or bad. Let it be, "The ability to monitor one’s own and others’ feelings and emotions, to discriminate among them and to use this information to guide one’s thinking and actions." Self-awareness,Self-regulation,Motivation,Empathy,Social skills. happiness may be an unavoidable side effect of cultivating emotional intelligence. Other side effects may include resilience, optimism, and kindness. “neuroplasticity.” The idea is that what we think, do, and pay attention to changes the structure and function of our brains. self-awareness depends on being able to see ourselves objectively, and that requires the ability to examine our thoughts and emotions from a third-person perspective. There is scientific evidence showing that improving our ability to regulate our attention can significantly impact how we respond to emotions. I think that looping in conversation would be a great practice in workplaces to avoid misunderstandings between peers and between leaders and their employees. Emotional awareness, accurate self-assessment and self confidence. I let my ego be simultaneously big and small, and I quietly laugh at its absurdity. That is a shy engineer’s secret to self-confidence.

How did you find the two minutes of mindfulness?


I found it a bit difficult to stop my mind from wandering when trying to practice mindfulness. If I try to focus on nothing for too long, my mind starts making lists of all the things I have to do which isn't that helpful. It will be something that I will have to actively practice.
