What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

In part, mindfulness is focus and control. Self awareness allows you to have focus and control because you know yourself. You know why you are doing something, or feeling something, and if you know that, you are better able to control what you are doing or how you are feeling - or at least respond in better ways to it. I liked the metaphor that, 'The moment you can see a raging river, it means you are already rising above it.' Knowledge is power and that's true about your control over yourself as well.

Minfulness provides you with the stabilty, clarity and focus to really look at yourself and see yourself objectively and with honesty to evaluate your strenghts, weaknesses and really understand why you react the way you do to certain things.


Body Scan - What was your experience of it? How did you feel?

I have been asked to do a similar process to focus on different parts of the body in order to relax and/or fall asleep before and I do find it can be very calming and relaxing. I do however find it difficult to shut off my mind, so I have to try really hard to focus on bringing it back when it wanders. Bringing a joyful memory to mind has an awesome effect on the body, it's surprising how much it changes your whole feeling about the moment when you remember something that brings you total happiness.


What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

I really liked the metaphor about the sky and the clouds. When trying to teach my children about positive ways to manage their emotions, I have spent a lot of time trying to analyse how different people react to and deal with their emotions and why. This section was really apt for me and something I will be sharing with my son because, especially when we get angry, we tend to think of emotions as defining us, particularly if we feel like we cannot control them. Beginning to look at them as something that pass, as simply a physiological experience that can be dealt with is a great step towards better emotional management.
