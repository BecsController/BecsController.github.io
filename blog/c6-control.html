When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

Running in to a problem when I completely don't think there should be one there causes a disproportionate emotional response for me. If I truly thought I had something right and I try it and it turns out that i've done something wrong it's difficult because I don't necessarily have an idea of where to start to correct the issue.


How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

I found the process quite helpful to actually identify where I feel negative emotion in my body. As the event that I had chosen to refelct on brought a strong emotional reaction, even considering it in the past tense, I was easily able to notice that anger tightens itself in my chest and that the hairs on my arms stood up just from thinking about it.
Just the act of concentrating enough on myself to notice where the anger was presenting in my body was enough to break some of the emotional response I was having. It was as if it allowed me to detach a bit more and see the event non-judgementally, as something that happened but didn't necessarily need to still affect me. I guess the difference between pain and aversion as the book talks about. It was an interesting process.
