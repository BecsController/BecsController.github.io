What is Scope and how does it work in Javascript?

There are two types of scope in JavaScript - local and global. Local scope means that the object is only available and readable within its function. Global scope means that it is available throughout the whole programme.

It's like if you were building a car and you wanted to apply certain characteristics to it, like number of wheels. If you defined a local variable, it would be like your own personal car that had five wheels. But a global scope would mean you could make car after car with three wheels but they wouldn't be personal to you they would be the same car over and over.
